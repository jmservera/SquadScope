## Wisdom Updates

### Keep

All existing heuristics remain sound and were not contradicted by W21 data:

- **Practical utility beats novelty theater** — validated by the skills/agent pattern repos gaining traction over broad agent launchers.
- **Clustered movement matters more than one loud launch** — the multiple "skills as agent behavior unit" repos this week is a textbook example of clustered movement.
- **Operational credibility is a strong positive signal** — `agent-quality-controls/slopless` is precisely the kind of operationally credible repo the heuristics favor; it was missed.
- **Stars without deltas are popularity, not momentum** — the "trending" section's reliance on cumulative star counts for freeCodeCamp et al. is exactly the failure this heuristic warns against.
- **Marketing-heavy wrappers are usually weak signal** — the 8+ Polymarket trading bot repos fit this pattern exactly.
- **Exploit, bypass, and cheat churn distort the picture** — gaming mod and cheat repos in "Notable New" confirmed this remains a live problem.
- **Look for absent infrastructure around known pain** — AI image provenance tooling gap is a concrete example.
- **Name what should exist but does not** — the gap section failed this test; the heuristic is correct.

- **"Be explicit about uncertainty" needs to extend to the correlation engine.** The current heuristic addresses prose uncertainty. Add: correlation match narratives should not proceed past confidence 0.7 without human review. The Microsoft/Google I/O misattribution is a direct consequence of narrating low-confidence name-matches as editorial conclusions.

Track this as a distinct category starting W22.

Output: repos flagged as `editorial-noise` (gaming mods, cheat tools, crypto bots, Roblox utilities) vs `review-needed`. Does not require AI.

Prevents the engine from narrating 0.6-confidence name-matches as editorial conclusions. Gate: only narrate correlations with confidence ≥ 0.75 and at least one non-org-name match.

Trigger: multiple repos in the same week with "skill", "agent harness", or "codex skill" in name/description. Output: categorize as `agentic-skills` and treat as clustered movement signal.

4. **`star-outlier-artifact-check`**: Pattern for flagging repos with unexpectedly high star counts relative to their apparent age or visibility. Useful for catching crawling artifacts or coordinated starring before they pollute editorial output.
